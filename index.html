About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Management Science and Engineering at the School of Management, Shanghai University, where I am expected to graduate in June 2027, under the supervision of Prof. Ying Ji. I hold a B.Sc. in Management Science from the University of Shanghai for Science and Technology (2022) and an M.Sc. in Management Science from Shanghai University (2024) as part of an integrated master's–Ph.D. programme.

My research centres on group decision-making, consensus reaching, and robust / distributionally robust optimisation under uncertainty. I am also interested in integrating computational intelligence and machine learning with group decision-making problems. My work has appeared in top-tier Q1 journals including Information Fusion (IF 14.1), Applied Soft Computing, and Information Sciences.

Supported by the China Scholarship Council (CSC) Fellowship (2025–2027), I am currently a visiting Ph.D. researcher at the University of Jaén, Spain, working under the joint supervision of Prof. Luis Martínez López, Prof. Bapi Dutta, and Prof. Rosa M. Rodríguez.

Research Interests

Group Decision-Making

Consensus reaching processes, expert opinion aggregation, and feedback mechanisms in multi-agent settings.

Robust Optimisation

Robust and distributionally robust models for decision problems with cost uncertainty and risk aversion.

Social Network Analysis

Opinion dynamics and trust-based consensus formation in large-scale social network group decision-making.

Uncertainty Quantification

Modelling ambiguity in expert preferences, adjustment costs, and failure-mode analysis under uncertainty.
